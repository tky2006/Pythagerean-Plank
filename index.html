How to Play

📋 Step-by-Step Guide

  1. 1Look at the right triangle — legs a and b are shown with their lengths.
  2. 2Choose the correct plank length from the 3 options below (only one is correct!).
  3. 3Use the formula a² + b² = c² to calculate the hypotenuse length.
  4. 4Drag the plank to the triangle — it will snap into place if correct.
  5. 5If the plank fits, you win the round! If not, you lose a life. ❤️

📐 Pythagorean Theorem Reference

a² + b² = c²
Example: If a = 3 and b = 4, then 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25, so c = √25 = 5
a and b are the two shorter legs forming the right angle.

c is the hypotenuse — always the longest side, opposite the right angle.
